        AquaWatch

        Satellite-powered water pollution detection for a healthier planet.

        Data Source

        Uses Sentinel-2 MSI imagery (10 m resolution) from the European Space Agency, accessed via Google Earth Engine. Data is updated every 5 days globally.

        Methodology

        • NDWI — Normalised Difference Water Index (McFeeters 1996): detects water presence and clarity.
        • NDTI — Normalised Difference Turbidity Index: measures suspended sediment and turbidity.
        • FAI — Floating Algae Index: detects algal blooms and surface scum.

        Classification

        StatusScoreMeaning
        Safe0–19Normal water quality
        Moderate20–49Elevated concern
        Polluted50+Significant pollution

        Tech Stack

        • Frontend: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, Leaflet.js, Plotly.js — deployed on Vercel
        • Backend: Python, FastAPI, Google Earth Engine API — deployed on Render
        • Imagery: Sentinel-2 SR via Google Earth Engine

        Disclaimer

        AquaWatch provides indicative water quality assessments based on remote sensing indices. Results should be validated with in-situ measurements before making public health or regulatory decisions. Cloud cover, atmospheric conditions, and seasonal variation can affect accuracy.
